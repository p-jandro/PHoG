# PHOG Question Drafts v2 — 2026-05-15

**Difficulty calibration:**
- **Easy** (~90% of casual fans get it) — `#00D4AA`
- **Medium** (~60% get it with thought) — `#0066FF`
- **Hard** (~25–30% — real knowledge) — `#FFA502`
- **Impossible** (<10% — deep cut, BTS, specialist) — `#FF4757`

Compared with v1, "impossible" tier now actually rewards specialist knowledge: behind-the-scenes details, exact stats, original creators (not popular ones), obscure factual reversals.

**New categories:** general gaming (retro + modern), fantasy films (Star Wars, Harry Potter, LOTR/Hobbit, other).

## TRUE / FALSE (45 statements, mixed difficulty)

Format: statement → answer + short explanation.

### Sports & motorsport

1. **The Puskás Aréna in Budapest will host the 2026 UEFA Champions League final.** → **TRUE.** Awarded by UEFA in 2023.

2. **Hungary won the 1954 World Cup, beating West Germany in the "Miracle of Bern."** → **FALSE.** Hungary *lost* that final 3–2 — the miracle was *for* West Germany.

3. **Flórián Albert is the only Hungarian footballer to have won the Ballon d'Or.** → **TRUE.** 1967.

4. **Ferencváros is the only Hungarian club ever to win a major European trophy.** → **TRUE.** 1964–65 Inter-Cities Fairs Cup, beating Juventus 1–0 in Turin.

5. **Hungary has not qualified for a FIFA World Cup since 1986.** → **TRUE.** Mexico '86 was the last appearance.

6. **Sebastian Vettel is still the youngest F1 World Champion of all time.** → **TRUE.** 23 y 134 d in 2010 — Verstappen and Hamilton didn't break it.

7. **Lewis Hamilton's first non-British team after 12 years at Mercedes was Ferrari, starting the 2025 season.** → **TRUE.**

8. **Ayrton Senna won the Monaco Grand Prix a record six times.** → **TRUE.** Five for McLaren consecutively + 1987 with Lotus.

9. **The 2021 Belgian Grand Prix is officially the shortest F1 race ever — only two laps behind the safety car.** → **TRUE.** Half points awarded.

10. **F1's Halo cockpit protection became mandatory in 2018.** → **TRUE.**

11. **The 2011 Canadian Grand Prix is the longest race in F1 history at over four hours.** → **TRUE.** Jenson Button won from last with six safety cars.

12. **Juan Manuel Fangio remains the oldest F1 World Champion at 46 years old.** → **TRUE.** Won in 1957.

### Geography

13. **Russia and China are the only two countries in the world that share land borders with 14 other countries.** → **TRUE.** Tied at 14 each.

14. **Hungary borders exactly eight other countries.** → **FALSE.** Seven: Austria, Slovakia, Ukraine, Romania, Serbia, Croatia, Slovenia.

15. **The Caspian Sea is technically the world's largest *lake*, though it is salty and endorheic.** → **TRUE.**

16. **France has more time zones than any other country in the world when its overseas territories are counted.** → **TRUE.** 12 time zones.

17. **Lake Baikal contains roughly 22% of all unfrozen surface freshwater on Earth.** → **TRUE.**

18. **Mount Kilimanjaro is the tallest mountain in Africa.** → **TRUE.**

19. **Mont Blanc is the tallest mountain in Europe and counts as one of the Seven Summits.** → **FALSE.** **Elbrus** (5,642 m) in Russia is the Seven Summits' European peak.

20. **Naypyidaw, not Yangon, has been Myanmar's official capital since 2005–2006.** → **TRUE.**

### Trackmania

21. **Trackmania Nations Forever has all seven environments (Stadium, Bay, Coast, Desert, Island, Rally, Snow).** → **FALSE.** Only Stadium — Trackmania *United* Forever has all seven.

22. **In Trackmania, the green "Author" medal means you matched or beat the time the map's creator set themselves.** → **TRUE.**

23. **TMNF was originally designed to be the official racing title of ESWC 2006.** → **TRUE.** That's why Nations was free.

24. **Trackmania (2020) was published by Ubisoft after the company acquired Nadeo in 2009.** → **TRUE.**

### Board games

25. **Monopoly's earliest patent was filed in 1903 by Charles Darrow.** → **FALSE.** Filed by Elizabeth "Lizzie" Magie. Darrow commercialized it in 1935.

26. **The "Free Parking jackpot" rule, where all paid fines pile up in the middle, is an official Hasbro rule.** → **FALSE.** It's a house rule; officially nothing happens on Free Parking.

27. **In Bang!, the Renegade only wins if they are the last player standing — and must personally land the killing blow on the Sheriff.** → **TRUE.**

28. **1s ("Pacos") are wild in standard Perudo rules and count as any face during normal rounds.** → **TRUE.**

29. **Hitster requires the players to have a Spotify account to scan the cards' QR codes.** → **TRUE.**

30. **Hitster was designed by a German game-design studio.** → **FALSE.** Designed by Swedish Marcus Carleson; later picked up by Jumbo (Netherlands).

### Hungarian places

31. **Budapest's Millennium Underground (today's M1) is the oldest electrified underground railway in continental Europe.** → **TRUE.** Opened 1896.

32. **Debrecen has been the capital of Hungary twice — in 1849 and in 1944.** → **TRUE.**

33. **Lake Tisza is one of Europe's largest natural lakes, formed at the end of the last Ice Age.** → **FALSE.** It's an artificial reservoir created by the Kisköre Dam (completed 1973).

34. **The Szántód–Tihany ferry is the shortest car-ferry crossing of Lake Balaton.** → **TRUE.**

35. **The Miskolctapolca Cave Bath is inside a natural karst cave — and its water is so low in minerals you can soak for hours.** → **TRUE.**

### Gaming

36. **The Konami Code (↑↑↓↓←→←→ B A) was first programmed into a port of the shoot-'em-up Gradius in 1986.** → **TRUE.** Kazuhisa Hashimoto put it there as a cheat and forgot to remove it.

37. **Counter-Strike began as a free Quake III mod.** → **FALSE.** Half-Life mod.

38. **Tetris was created in the Soviet Union by Alexey Pajitnov in 1984.** → **TRUE.**

39. **The PlayStation 2 is the best-selling home console of all time at around 160 million units.** → **TRUE.**

40. **Hideo Kojima directed Metal Gear Solid (1998) for PS1.** → **TRUE.**

### Fantasy films / books

41. **The Lord of the Rings: The Return of the King won every single Academy Award it was nominated for — 11 of 11.** → **TRUE.** Tied Ben-Hur and Titanic in count, but uniquely a clean sweep.

42. **The original Star Wars (A New Hope) won the Academy Award for Best Picture in 1978.** → **FALSE.** *Annie Hall* won. Star Wars won six technical Oscars but not Best Picture.

43. **The Hobbit was published in 1937, almost 20 years before The Lord of the Rings.** → **TRUE.** LOTR came 1954–55.

44. **House of the Dragon is based on George R.R. Martin's stand-alone book *Fire & Blood* (2018), not the main A Song of Ice and Fire novels.** → **TRUE.**

45. **Richard Harris played Dumbledore in all eight Harry Potter films.** → **FALSE.** He played Dumbledore in films 1 and 2 only, then died in October 2002. Michael Gambon took over from film 3.

## DESIGN NOTES

**Quiz difficulty curve check.** Sample expected-correct rates:
- Easy: Vader = Anakin, Mario in Donkey Kong, 7 HP books, Frodo carries ring → ~95% correct.
- Medium: Snape's Patronus, Rian Johnson directed VIII, OG won TI 2018 & 2019 → ~55–65%.
- Hard: Senna died at Tamburello, Schlosser's top-scorer streak, Two Towers per Tolkien → ~25–35%.
- Impossible: Baghetti 1961 French GP, Fridwulfa, "Obi-Wan killed your father" decoy line, Alamogordo, Faker's 6th title → real <10% rate.

**True/False distribution:** ~30% counter-intuitive flips (Sahara isn't largest desert, FTC's Hidegkuti hat-trick), ~50% true statements that *sound* surprising, ~20% obvious checks. Mix discourages "always answer true."

**Pointless tuning.** Scores assume a Hungarian 30-something pool. Note especially:
- P10 (Hogwarts founders) and P11 (Horcruxes) are short rounds — better as bonus or 30-second sprint than full rounds.
- P13 (100M+ game franchises) is the deepest list — strong main-event round.
- P14 (PS home consoles) — accept "PS4 Slim" only as PS4; same for other slims unless explicitly listed.

**Open swaps if anything's stale:**
- Faker's title count in R8 may grow before games ship; check before deploy.
- Andor S2 line in research has 2025 date which is current — verify CTA wording in any Andor question.
- Hitster designer correction stands: Carleson, not Engel.

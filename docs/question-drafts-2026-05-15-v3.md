# PHOG Question Drafts v3 — 2026-05-15

**Difficulty calibration:**
- **Easy** (`#00D4AA`) ~90% — fans of the topic get it
- **Medium** (`#0066FF`) ~60% — most recall with thought
- **Hard** (`#FFA502`) ~25-30% — real genre knowledge
- **Impossible** (`#FF4757`) <10% — counter-intuitive, BTS, deep specialist

**v3 changes from v2:** rounds now mix four different categories (one per difficulty), matching the original `quizRounds.json` style. Impossibles redesigned so the *distractors are also plausible* — pure random facts have been swapped for counter-intuitive twists, behind-the-scenes details, and "the famous version is wrong" reveals.

Each "impossible" was sanity-checked against: would 9 of 10 informed players pick a wrong answer? If a distractor is so weak it's eliminated by elimination, the question becomes easy. All four options below should *look* possible to a knowledgeable player.

## TRUE / FALSE (45 statements)

Mixed across all categories. ~30% are counter-intuitive flips designed to punish "always answer true."

1. **The Puskás Aréna in Budapest will host the 2026 UEFA Champions League final.** → **TRUE.**

2. **Hungary won the 1954 World Cup with a victory over West Germany in the "Miracle of Bern."** → **FALSE.** Hungary lost that final 3–2 — the miracle was *for* the Germans.

3. **Flórián Albert is the only Hungarian to have won the Ballon d'Or.** → **TRUE.** 1967.

4. **Ferencváros is the only Hungarian club to have won a major European trophy.** → **TRUE.** 1964–65 Inter-Cities Fairs Cup, beating Juventus 1–0 in Turin.

5. **Sebastian Vettel remains the youngest F1 World Champion in history.** → **TRUE.** 23 y 134 d in 2010.

6. **Lewis Hamilton's first non-British team after 12 years at Mercedes was Ferrari, starting the 2025 season.** → **TRUE.**

7. **The 2021 Belgian Grand Prix is the shortest race in F1 history — basically two laps behind the safety car.** → **TRUE.** Half points awarded.

8. **Ayrton Senna won the Monaco Grand Prix a record six times.** → **TRUE.**

9. **Russia and China each share land borders with 14 different countries — the joint world record.** → **TRUE.**

10. **Hungary borders exactly eight other countries.** → **FALSE.** Seven: Austria, Slovakia, Ukraine, Romania, Serbia, Croatia, Slovenia.

11. **Mont Blanc is the European peak in the Seven Summits.** → **FALSE.** It's **Elbrus** in the Caucasus.

12. **France has more time zones than any other country once overseas territories are counted.** → **TRUE.** 12 in total.

13. **Lake Baikal contains roughly 22% of all unfrozen surface freshwater on Earth.** → **TRUE.**

14. **TMNF was originally designed to be the official racing title of ESWC 2006 in Paris.** → **TRUE.** That's why it was free.

15. **Trackmania Nations Forever ships with all seven Trackmania environments.** → **FALSE.** Only Stadium — the seven-environment game is Trackmania *United* Forever.

16. **The green "Author" medal in Trackmania means you matched or beat the time the map's creator set.** → **TRUE.**

17. **Monopoly's earliest patent was filed in 1903 by Charles Darrow.** → **FALSE.** Filed by Elizabeth "Lizzie" Magie. Darrow commercialised it in 1935.

18. **The "Free Parking jackpot" — where paid fines pool up in the middle — is an official Monopoly rule.** → **FALSE.** It's a house rule. Officially nothing happens on Free Parking.

19. **In Bang!, the Renegade wins only if they're last alive and they personally deal the killing blow to the Sheriff.** → **TRUE.**

20. **Hitster requires a Spotify account to scan and play the song cards.** → **TRUE.**

21. **Hitster's designer is a Swedish indie creator who launched it via Kickstarter before Jumbo took it worldwide.** → **TRUE.** Marcus Carleson.

22. **Budapest's M1 yellow line, opened in 1896, is the oldest electrified underground railway in continental Europe.** → **TRUE.**

23. **Lake Tisza is one of Europe's largest natural lakes, formed by glacial activity.** → **FALSE.** It's an artificial reservoir created by the Kisköre Dam (completed 1973).

24. **Debrecen has been the capital of Hungary twice — in 1849 and in 1944.** → **TRUE.**

25. **The Szántód–Tihany car ferry is the shortest crossing on Lake Balaton.** → **TRUE.**

26. **Hungary's first nuclear reactor went critical at Paks in 1959.** → **FALSE.** It went critical at the KFKI in **Csillebérc** in 1959 (Paks didn't come online until 1982).

27. **Tetris was invented by an American developer in 1984.** → **FALSE.** Soviet engineer Alexey Pajitnov, on a Soviet Elektronika 60 computer.

28. **The PlayStation 2 is the best-selling home console of all time at around 160 million units.** → **TRUE.**

29. **Counter-Strike began as a free mod for the original Half-Life in 1999.** → **TRUE.**

30. **The Konami Code first appeared in a NES port of *Gradius* in 1986.** → **TRUE.**

31. **Hideo Kojima directed Metal Gear Solid (1998).** → **TRUE.**

32. **The first known Easter egg in a console video game was hidden in Atari's *Adventure* (1980) by programmer Warren Robinett.** → **TRUE.**

33. **The original *Star Wars* (A New Hope) won the Academy Award for Best Picture in 1978.** → **FALSE.** *Annie Hall* did. Star Wars won 6 technical Oscars but lost Best Picture.

34. **The Lord of the Rings: The Return of the King won every Oscar it was nominated for — 11 out of 11.** → **TRUE.**

35. **Tolkien was born in England.** → **FALSE.** He was born in **Bloemfontein**, in what's now South Africa.

36. **The Hobbit was published in 1937, almost 20 years before *The Lord of the Rings*.** → **TRUE.**

37. **House of the Dragon is based on Martin's *Fire & Blood* (2018), not the main A Song of Ice and Fire books.** → **TRUE.**

38. **The HBO show is set roughly 200 years before *Game of Thrones*.** → **TRUE.**

39. **Richard Harris played Dumbledore in all eight Harry Potter films.** → **FALSE.** Films 1–2 only; he died in October 2002. Michael Gambon took over from film 3.

40. **Harry Potter himself is one of Voldemort's Horcruxes — though made unintentionally.** → **TRUE.**

41. **In *Lord of the Rings*, the Two Towers of the title's middle volume are Orthanc and Barad-dûr, as the films imply.** → **FALSE.** Tolkien's own note says **Orthanc and Minas Morgul.**

42. **Andrzej Sapkowski sold the rights to the Witcher video games to CD Projekt Red for a one-time flat fee and missed out on royalties.** → **TRUE.** (Later renegotiated.)

43. **The Lion, the Witch and the Wardrobe was the first published Narnia book, in 1950.** → **TRUE.** (Though chronologically internal-order starts with *The Magician's Nephew*.)

44. **Faker has won six League of Legends World Championships as of the 2025 season.** → **TRUE.**

45. **The OG Dota 2 team's TI8 prize haul was over $11 million.** → **TRUE.**

## QUALITY CHECK ON IMPOSSIBLES

Each impossible was reviewed against the rule "all four options must look plausible to a knowledgeable player." Reasoning per round:

| R | Question | Why distractors hold up |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Riolu cheating exposure | All four (Pac, Spam, Riolu, Bren) are well-known TM names — guessers can't eliminate |
| 2 | Tolkien's birthplace | Edinburgh / Mumbai (his father was bank-bound for South Africa) / Auckland all sound plausible for an English-language fantasy author |
| 3 | E.T. burial town | All four are real New Mexico towns; Roswell is the obvious "famous NM" decoy |
| 4 | ESB decoy line | "I am your uncle" and "Yoda is your father" are joke-plausible; "Obi-Wan is your brother" is the strongest decoy |
| 5 | Fridwulfa | All four are pseudo-Norse / Welsh-sounding names — no giveaway |
| 6 | Nepal UTC+5:45 | All four are real Asian neighbours of India with non-standard time zones plausible |
| 7 | 1965 Fairs Cup goalscorer (Fenyvesi) | Albert (real FTC star), Varga and Vincze (era-correct distractors) |
| 8 | Baghetti | All four are real F1 names from the era |
| 9 | Carleson (Hitster) | "Sebastian Engel" is the brief's wrong answer, Teuber/Knizia are German titans — all sound right |
| 10 | TMNF Exchange track count (~640K) | All four numbers feel plausible — casual players underestimate, mega-fans guess too high. 640K is genuinely surprising |
| 11 | Hashimoto (Konami Code) | All four are famous Japanese game devs — Yamauchi was Nintendo, Sakurai Smash, Igarashi Castlevania |
